I do not believe in hell

One of my favourite things to do for a long time has been to read about the beliefs systems of various religions. And it has been immensely rewarding intellectually, from being inspired by the corporal and spiritual works of mercy in christianity, the idea of "form is no different from void; void is no different from form" expressed in heart sutra, the unbelievable declaration of "I am the absolute truth; And verily everything is the same absolute truth" claimed by advaita tradition, etc.
But one thing I simply can not digest, conceive or comprehend is the existence of hell.
Specifically, the kind of hell vividly described in many major religions, where offenders are tormented by fire, brimstone, various creatures, tortured by various means, etc. The reason is simple : I have a firm faith in the merciful and loving nature of the creator. Thus, I am unable to fathom the utility the all perfect creator would gain by inflicting agony on his own children.
Even in mundane matters, we find forgiveness and firm yet meaningful instruction to be more effective at changing someone's behaviour rather than anger or any expression of harshness. There seems to be no positive to have people writhe in despair.
And as far as the argument goes "some people should be punished severely for their actions", it completely falls apart when we see the creator as our "parent". No parent who is emotionally stable would ever want their children to suffer for eternity, even if such a child has committed unspeakable acts. For that is what it means to be someone's "parent"; that is the reason most traditions resign themselves to describe a mother's love as "immeasurable".
Except, there seems to be a certain utility to such an idea of hell floating around, even if it seems barbaric : it discourages 'some' people from being evil. There seem to be people who argue that without hell, there would be simply no motivation to be ethical; for such people, the "idea" of hell is beneficial, similar to how a parent tells their children a "white lie" to protect them. But I simply refuse to accept that the creator can conceive a place of eternal damnation.
Now, I do believe in hell, but it is neither permanent nor a place we reach in afterlife. We see so many people suffer from external diseases or internal turmoil of emotions like rage, guilt, regret, envy, fear, etc. I believe such a condition is what hell truly entails.
In my eyes, hell (and heaven, for that matter), seems to be here on earth.
Everything else involving dedicated centres of crude torture seems to resemble more of the human nature to torment and inflict harm than anything else.
