Why do we bother to write?

We write to record, to journal, to quote later, to practice something we have learnt, etc. But, the common theme would be that we write, to remember. To not be forgotten.
But what is it that we wish to preserve? Is it our own fleeting existence, or might it be even more transient existence of our thoughts? Indeed, they say, you can kill someone, but not their ideas; if so, certainly putting in the effort to preserve what we think makes sense.
However, it is certainly possible to preserve things without writing them down, however tedious it may be. Apart from visual construction, which would be a trivial example to the claim, the collective memory of humans has been utilised to preserve things with unimaginable fidelity (the example I have at the top of my head would be Rig veda hymns).
So let's reframe our reasoning for writing : it helps us to not be forgotten conveniently. Sure, we can all have elaborate techniques to memorise, but beyond a certain volume of information, we end up with a headache, which is inconvenient. And like the universe tends towards the state of high entropy, we gravitate towards convenience. If you think about it, it is most certainly possible for me to journal all this on a piece of paper. But it is convenient to type. I can correct my mistakes, revisit and rewrite my arguments with ease. And, I can share it with everyone in a way I can't share a piece of paper, much less my memory.
And that brings me to my final reason why we write : to share. Share conveniently, but also share reliably. My words could be distorted knowingly or unknowingly like the good old game of chinese whisper. It's harder to distort something I've written down. DO NOT BE MISTAKEN, it is possible (let me quickly add the cliche quote of "impossible" has "possible" in it, possibly given by someone who hasn't had the opportunity to learn about the usage of prefixes), but it is far less probable.
So, we write to not be forgotten and disseminate conveniently and reliably. If you think about it, the progress of technology, from engraving on pillars to error correcting codes, it is in the direction of increasing the reliability and convenience.
